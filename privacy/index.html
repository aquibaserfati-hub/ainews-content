PRIVACY POLICY

AINews Weekly

Last updated April 30, 2026.

What we collect

Almost nothing. AINews Weekly v2 added an AI tutor that sends your messages to Anthropic (see "AI tutor" below). Everything else is unchanged: no accounts, no logins, no analytics SDKs, no crash reporting, no advertising trackers, no third-party data brokers.

What's stored on your device

The app stores these things locally on your iPhone, not on our servers:

  • Cached weekly digest — the publicly published digest JSON. No information about you.
  • Cached lesson curriculum — the publicly published lesson content JSON. No information about you.
  • Your bookmarks — which Learn items you saved, in iOS UserDefaults. We never see it.
  • Your lesson progress — which steps you've completed, timestamps, in iOS UserDefaults. We never see it.
  • Your tutor transcript — messages sent to the tutor and its replies, in iOS UserDefaults. We never see it. (Messages travel to Anthropic when sent — see "AI tutor" below.)
  • A per-install token — an opaque random UUID in iOS Keychain, used to rate-limit tutor requests. Does not identify you personally. We never see it.

All of the above clear when you uninstall the app.

AI tutor

When you use "Ask the tutor," your message and the current lesson context are sent to Anthropic via a proxy we run on Cloudflare Workers. This is the only data AINews Weekly transmits off-device about you.

What we send:

  • Your typed message
  • Current lesson title, step title, and step content (for context)
  • Which steps you've already completed in this lesson
  • An opaque per-install token for rate limiting (not linked to your identity or Apple ID)

We do not store your tutor messages on our servers. The Cloudflare Worker processes requests statelessly; Anthropic returns a reply. Anthropic's data handling is governed by their privacy policy. The transcript is stored in UserDefaults on your device only.

How content reaches your phone

The digest and curriculum are published as public JSON files at aquibaserfati-hub.github.io/ainews-content/ by GitHub Actions. GitHub (the host) sees your IP as part of normal HTTPS traffic. We do not have access to those server logs.

Links to external sites

Tapping a source link, external URL, or "Watch the video" button opens that site in Safari or the YouTube app. That site's privacy policy applies from that point.

Children's privacy

AINews Weekly does not intentionally collect data from children under 13. Because the AI tutor sends user messages to Anthropic, children under 13 should not use the tutor feature.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the "last updated" date above will change. Any material change would relate to the AI tutor feature (the only off-device data path).
